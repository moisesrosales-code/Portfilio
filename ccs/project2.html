Stormy Dinner Party

A short video study focused on movement, atmosphere, and observation.

For this project, I made a video in Adobe Premiere Pro that captures what it feels like to be at a dinner party during a storm. Instead of using dialogue or narration, I focused on sound and visuals to create the atmosphere. I combined background music, snippets of conversation, and storm sounds like wind and rain to draw viewers into the scene.

While looking for images and footage, I chose shots that matched the mood of the story, making sure every clip added to the experience. I tried out more effects this time, like warping and distortion, to highlight the storm’s instability and tension. These techniques made the mood stronger and helped the setting feel more real.

This project showed me how sound and visual effects work together to tell a story. By using only Premiere Pro, I got more comfortable with its tools and saw how my editing choices could affect how the audience feels.
