About

Bio

Moises Rosales is a San Jose State University student majoring in Digital Media Art and is an aspiring artist. Animation, video games, comic books, and anything involving hand or digital art attracted his interest. Although he spent most of his early years drawing, he took a break in middle school, so he didn't use the theme to practice and improve his skills then. However, he returned to art near the end of high school, and now, because of his interest in digital technology, he has pursued a career in which he can express himself across various fields of art and digital media.

Artist Statement

I have always been able to rely on art as a source of comfort during times of uncertainty. Since I was a child, I have written and drawn countless characters, locations, and scenarios simply for the sake of creating, regardless of whether they looked exactly like I had envisioned them. Whether I drew with pencils and paper in high school or developed my computer-aided skills in college, I have consistently enjoyed bringing my concepts to life. Although my medium may have changed over time, my method has remained consistent.

I began to look at art online, and this inspired me to express myself through perspectives and tell stories through art, which is still a passion of mine today. When I initially applied to San Jose State University, I selected Animation as my first option and Digital Media Art as my second. I was not accepted into the Animation major; however, I was accepted into the DMA major. Honestly, this was not what I expected; yet, it ultimately became beneficial because it not only provided me with the outlets I desired, but it also required me to consider art in ways I had previously not considered. Through my coursework, I came to understand that technology was not a tool separate from art, but rather, technology is another form of expression, allowing me to develop upon the previous interests I had.

It was ART 74 where many of these connections occurred for me. I was introduced to and utilized Adobe Photoshop, Adobe Illustrator, and experimented with AI-generated images; however, the greatest thing I learned in that class was how to create my first web-based portfolio site from scratch, utilizing HTML and CSS. My initial site was essentially just one scrolling page with all of my various works layered on top of one another. While the initial site functioned, it did not provide a complete view of all of the things I desired to show. Over time, as I worked on my own, I revised it into a multi-page site with separate sections dedicated to my digital art and animation, videography, and fine art, each having its own layout and navigation. This process of evolving my portfolio site is among the clearest indicators of my growth as a digital artist.

In ART 75, I was given the opportunity to delve much further into storytelling and technology. For the first assignment, I created a video depicting a tennis ball traveling throughout the SJSU campus, taking in the sights and visiting the library. I wanted the video to reflect both a mood and a narrative, so the viewer would feel the tennis ball's curiosity and personification. It was my first time utilizing Adobe After Effects, where I isolated the ball using rotoscoping and added an echo effect to enhance the sense of movement and life. In my final project, I once again utilized the tennis ball and took it to a local park, where it slid down slides, rolled through open areas, and traveled through a playful and dreamlike environment. I emphasized the music, continued to utilize the echo effect, and concentrated on creating seamless and cohesive transitions between shots. Both of these assignments taught me that the same subject matter can evoke vastly different emotions based on how it is used, and I consider this when developing each new project.

My future goals include continuing to grow in the areas of creative coding, video, interactive media, and visual design. My long-term goal is to establish myself as a Multimedia Designer and create content that incorporates art, technology, and storytelling to impact and engage the audience. The BFA program will be the next stage in establishing my practice, and I am prepared to put in the effort.
